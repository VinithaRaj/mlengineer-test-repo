Describe access control model.

Access control models are defines who can access which data, and what they can do with the data they access. The 3 main types of access control models are:
i. Role based access control - where access to a resource is limited by the user's role in the organisation such as departments or hierarchical levels.
ii. Attribute based access control - where access to a resource is limited by attributes of a resource, user, and environment such as some users are given access to confidential files and they can be from different departments, as long as their attributes match their policy. This model is easier for fine grained control.
iii. Access Control List - where access to a resource is limited by what is specified for that specific resource, and is therefore very precise.
